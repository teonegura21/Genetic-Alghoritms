Detailed Insights on GA Implementation

  Let me analyze the GA implementation in great depth from the GeneticAlgorithm.h file:

  1. Hybrid Evolutionary Strategy with Generational Replacement
  The GA implements a sophisticated generational replacement model with 200+200 strategy:
   - Population: 200 individuals (at limit after selection)
   - Offspring: 200 individuals (100% of population = 50% elitism)
   - Total during evolution: 400 individuals → select best 200 (50% elitism)
   - Protected elite: top 20% never replaced during diversity injection

  2. Advanced Crossover Operators
  The implementation uses three distinct crossover operators:

  A. Greedy Edge Crossover (Custom):
   - At each step, chooses the shorter edge from either parent
   - Creates the "best" child by combining good edges from both parents
   - Algorithm: Start from random city, at each step select next city from either parent that creates shorter edge
   - Fallback: If both suggestions from parents are already visited, finds nearest unvisited city

  B. Order Crossover (OX):
   - Standard crossover for TSP
   - Copies a segment from parent1, fills rest from parent2 in order
   - Preserves relative ordering of cities

  C. Random Mix Crossover (Innovative):
   - For each position, randomly pick from parent A or B (50% chance each)
   - Then fixes duplicates to ensure valid Hamiltonian cycle
   - Step 1: For each position, try to pick from A or B (50% chance), prioritizing unused cities
   - Step 2: Find missing cities that weren't placed
   - Step 3: Fill empty positions with missing cities in random order

  3. Sophisticated Mutation and Diversity Mechanisms
   - Inversion Mutation: Reverses a random segment (equivalent to 2-opt move)
   - Swap Mutation: Exchanges two random cities
   - Adaptive Mutation Rate: Dynamically adjusts from 0.25 base between 0.10 (floor) and 0.9 (ceiling)
   - Stagnation Response: When no improvement for 10 generations, aggressively increases mutation rate
   - Hard Restart: After 40 generations without improvement, keeps top 5%, replaces rest with random

  4. Multi-Level Diversity Control
  The GA employs several diversity preservation mechanisms:

  A. Immigrant Injection:
   - Every 15 generations, replaces 10% of population with random individuals
   - Protects top 10% (20 individuals) from replacement

  B. Variance-Collapse Detection:
   - If population standard deviation < 1e-3, replaces bottom 25% (50 individuals) with random
   - Increases mutation rate to 0.5 when detecting convergence

  C. Stagnation-Driven Diversity:
   - After 10 generations without improvement: replaces bottom 30% (60 individuals)
   - Alternates between random immigrants and heavily mutated good individuals (5 mutations each)

  5. Adaptive Parameter Control
   - Mutation Rate Scheduling: Increases when stuck, decreases when improving (0.95 multiplier when improving)
   - Local Search Scheduling: Begins at generation 200, reduces probability for larger instances, further reduces in final 20% of generations

  6. Hybrid Integration with Local Search
   - 2-opt Improvement: Applied to children with probability 0.25 after burn-in (200 generations)
   - 2-opt Full Optimization: Applied periodically to best solution (every 500 generations for small instances, every 1000 for large)
   - Lin-Kernighan Polish: Applied to top individuals periodically, final LK polish on best solution
   - LK Scheduling: Frequency and intensity adjusted based on problem size (fewer calls for large instances)

  7. Advanced Survivor Selection
   - Sorts entire combined population (parents + offspring) by fitness
   - Keeps only the best 200 individuals
   - Maintains top individuals while allowing exploration

  8. Comprehensive Statistics and Early Termination
   - Tracks best, mean, worst distances and standard deviation per generation
   - Early Termination Options:
     - Gap threshold: Stops if within 2% of known optimal
     - Stagnation: Stops after 300 generations without improvement
   - Convergence Analysis: Tracks improvement patterns in first vs second half of run

  9. Problem-Size Adaptive Strategies
   - For larger instances (>500 cities): Reduces local search probability by 50% to save time
   - For very large instances (>1000 cities): Further reduces LK frequency and applies to fewer individuals
   - Final generation LK: Applies less frequently for larger instances to maintain computational efficiency

  10. Innovative Restart and Recovery Mechanisms
   - Protected Elite + Diversity Injection: After stagnation, protects top 20%, replaces bottom 30%, alternating between random immigrants and heavily mutated good individuals
   - Hard Restart: Keeps top 5% after prolonged stagnation, intensifies exploration
